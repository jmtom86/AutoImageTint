QHow long before I can roll down my windows?

ADepending on weather conditions you can roll down your window after 24 hours of installation, however, note that it may take between 3-5 days for the film to completely dry out.

QWhy do I see little bubbles and haze in the film immediately after installation?

AThis is normal. We use a water solution to activate the adhesive which allow us to move the film into position. Although we squeegee out as much of the water as possible, a small amount of water remains between the glass and film. As the water evaporates through the film you may see little water bubbles of foggy/hazy patches. This should clear up as the water completely evaporates (between 3-5 days) or longer if the weather is cold or raining.

QWhat do I use to clean my windows?

AYou can clean your windows with soap and warm water or alcohol and water. Do not use ammonia, chemicals or abrasive cleaners as it can scratch or deteriorate your film.

QHow durable is the film?

AWindow film is protected by a hard scratch–resistant coating that can withstand everyday use. Although the film is "scratch- resistant" this does not mean "scratch–proof." Even glass can be scratched, so be careful when loading and unloading objects with sharp edge such as bikes, boxes and sports equipment. Also be careful when releasing your seat belt quickly as the buckle can hit your window and may cause small nicks in your film that is not covered by your warranty.

QWill the film damage my defrost lines?

AThis depends on how long the film has been on the car. In most cases, it will not damage the defrost lines if you remove the film. However, in some cases, on some vehicles, it does damage the defrost lines. In any case, we do not guarantee when we remove the film that it will not damage the defrost lines.

QWhat is the warranty on window film?

AThe films we carry come with a lifetime warranty against bubbling, peeling and fading. You will need your warranty card and receipt to claim any warranty issues.

QHow is Suntek Paint Protection Film maintained?

AOnce applied, refrain from washing the vehicle in an automated car wash for 48 hours. Washing after that time should present no problem. Follow the care instructions on the warranty card.

QWhat is the best way to clean Suntek Paint Protection Film?

AHand washing with soap and water is recommended. We do not recommend using any harsh chemicals to remove any bird dropping residue. Do not use a pressure washer on the film. It can damage or lift the film off the painted surface.

QCan I use wax over Suntek Paint Protection Film?

AYes. Every time you wax your paint, wax your film. When waxing the vehicle, use spray wax on the paint protection film instead of liquid wax to prevent wax build up along the films edge. This can easily be removed with a dry soft cloth. Do not use pigmented or abrasive cleaners, waxes, polishes or rubbing compounds on the film.

QWhat is the warranty on the Suntek Paint Protection film?

AThe film has a 5 year warranty against bubbling and cracking and a 2 year warranty on labor.

QCan Suntek Paint Protection Film be applied over a vehicle that has been repainted or touched up?

ASuntek recommends that any repainted surfaces on your vehicle be professionally painted and fully cured before applying paint protection film. If you have touched up scratches or chips with a tube of "touch-up" paint and then apply paint protection film over it, the film will protect that area until it is removed. If the car was properly prepped and painted, you should have no problem. In some cases if the vehicle was not properly painted, the clear bra may pull the paint off during the installation process in which case, we are not responsible.
